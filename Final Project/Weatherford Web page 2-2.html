Heartland 2024 Cruise Car Show

Sittin’ and watchin’ Friday night’s car cruise was relaxin’, as was walkin’ around Rader Park on Saturday, hearin’ the Loud Exhaust Contest, observin’ the Burnout Contest and viewin’ cars, pick-up trucks and motorcycles in the Car Show, all part of Weatherford’s 34th Annual Cruise Car Show, June 14-15. The event was free to the public. Long-time organizers Michelle Lee, Joanne Phain and Tommy Nabors estimated 5,500 people attended.

On Friday night, two thousand folks watched as 200 antique, classic and newer vehicles paraded downtown on Main Street (Route 66) and around SWOSU in a closed loop motorcade.

On Saturday, Rader Park filled up quickly with at least 150 car show participants, 88 vendors and about 3,500 attendees.Engines growled, whirled and shrieked with deafening pop-pop-pops as 15 vehicles tried to outdo each other in the Loud Exhaust Contest.

At 2:00 p.m., 20 vehicles tried to produce the thickest white clouds of smoke in the Burnout Contest.

Another successful event? Yes, said Michelle Lee, Lead Planner. “I think our attendance, all the way around, is up,” referring to total participation.

The event was hosted by the Weatherford Rotary Club and Heartland Cruise Car Show Committee.
